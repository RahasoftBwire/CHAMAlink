The Spark: Grandmother's Wisdom

It was 2007, during the challenging siku za Moi era, when I first witnessed the magic of a Chama. I was just 8 years old then. My grandmother, Joyce Mwato, a retired teacher in rural Kenya, would gather with twelve other women every Saturday morning. They called themselves "Bidii Women's Group" - Bidii meaning effort in Swahili.

As a curious 8-year-old, I watched my grandmother walk miles to sit under a large acacia tree with other women, each carrying a small envelope. What I witnessed changed my understanding of community forever. Each woman contributed 100 Kenyan Shillings - a significant amount for rural families then, especially during those difficult economic times. That experience made me think about technology every day.

In 2010, when I was 11, my mother Judith Lily joined my grandmother's chama. Watching both of them walk those long distances to attend meetings broke my heart. They never gave up, despite the challenges. Their dedication inspired me to dream of making life simpler for every mother out there.

"Mwana wange, when women come together with purpose, mountains move."

My grandmother, Joyce Mwato

Within two years, I watched my grandmother transform our family's financial situation. She used her Chama payout to start a small kiosk, which eventually funded my education and changed our family's trajectory forever. Every day since then, I couldn't stop thinking about how technology could make this process easier for families like ours.

Years later, at Mount Kenya University pursuing my computer science degree, I carried this vision with me. I really felt bad remembering my grandmother and mother walking miles just to attend meetings. I knew there had to be a better way. Now, at 25 years old, I've created this beautiful app that will help every chama across Africa.

02

The Education: Building the Foundation

Thanks to my grandmother's Chama, I was able to pursue computer science at Mount Kenya University. But even as I learned about algorithms and databases, my mind kept returning to that acacia tree and those Saturday meetings.

During university, I started a small tech consultancy with classmates. We built websites for local businesses, but I noticed a pattern - many of our clients were asking for systems to manage group savings and loans. The demand for digitizing Chamas was real.

The Problem I Saw
  • Manual record-keeping prone to errors
  • Lack of transparency in financial dealings
  • Difficulty tracking contributions and loans
  • Limited access to financial insights
The Vision I Had
  • Digital-first Chama management
  • Real-time financial transparency
  • Mobile accessibility for all members
  • Data-driven decision making
03

The Journey: From Idea to Reality

After graduation, I spent five years working for various fintech companies across Kenya and Nigeria. I learned about mobile money, blockchain, and financial inclusion. But every project I worked on made me realize how underserved the Chama market was.

In 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, I witnessed Chamas struggling to meet physically. Many disbanded because they couldn't manage their finances remotely. That's when I knew the time had come to build Bwire Finance Cloud.

March 2020 - The Pivot

Left my corporate job to build Bwire Finance Cloud full-time

August 2020 - First MVP

Launched with 10 Chamas in Nairobi and Mombasa

January 2021 - Major Milestone

Reached 100 Chamas and KES 10M in managed funds

December 2023 - Going Enterprise

Launched enterprise features for SACCOs and NGOs
